Kahoot! : What Do You Know About Anxiety?

Purpose

This presentation is to raise awareness in stopping teenage anxiety and educate students about its signs, symptoms, and treatment.

Match:

Get into groups of 4. In the next slide, you will be given a series of phrases that may or may not be common signs of anxiety. Phrases:

  • Feeling guilty/sad
  • Little or too much appetite
  • Muscle Tension
  • Breath Shortages
  • High Blood Pressure
  • Nausea
  • Blood clots
  • Feeling worthless
  • Frequent sweating

Answers:

Signs:

  • Muscle Tension
  • Breath Shortages
  • Nausea
  • Frequent sweating

Not Signs:

  • Feeling guilty/sad
  • Little or too much appetitie
  • High Blood Pressure
  • Blood clots
  • Feeling worthless

Talk:

Talk to your group and figure out some possible symptoms of anxiety. Do this for 5 minutes. After 5 minutes, your instructor will read to you what is correct.

Answer:

The symptoms are the same as the signs

Treatment:

Talk to friends, family, teachers, and counselors. Let them know what is going on in your life. They will do their best to help you.

Also, try consulting staff at the Wellness Center. They are always willing to give you a helping hand.

You can even try contacting Huckleberry Youth Programs, a nonprofit organization where you can talk to a trained professional about your feelings. They'll be able to help you! It is free. They are available 24/7.

Their phone number is 1-415-621-2929

Treatment (Continued):

Anxiey can even be further treated with the right food choices and exercise

You can use yoga and deep-breathing practices to calm yourself

Eating healthier, getting enough exercise, and sleeping better can also lead to a calmer day.

Refraining from coffee, smoking, alcohol, and drugs can reduce the possibility of panic attacks.
